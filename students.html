The Student Experience

Frustration, resilience, and the demand for relevance.

A Disconnect in the Classroom

Young Tunisians often express deep frustration with the education system's relevance. Surveys consistently find that students prefer learning that builds critical thinking and practical skills rather than the traditional rote memorization that still dominates many classrooms.

"Future educational reforms should first consider what students want to become… even if it doesn’t match the classic path."

The Burden of Tutoring

For many, public school is just the beginning of the day. Private tutoring has become a "nightmare" for families, draining budgets and exhausting students. In regions like Kairouan, parents have even blocked roads to protest the lack of teachers, desperate for their children to have a chance.

What Students Want

  • ✅ Critical Thinking: Moving away from memorization.
  • ✅ Practical Skills: Preparation for the modern job market.
  • ✅ A Voice: Inclusion in the reform process.
