The Educators' Reality

Understaffed, undervalued, and fighting for respect.

A Profession in Crisis

Teachers and university lecturers in Tunisia report demoralizing conditions. Once a highly respected profession, many now feel it suffers from low respect and heavy burdens.

"156,000 secondary school teachers... suffering from ‘a slow bleeding.’ They no longer see it as a respected, fulfilling profession."

University Struggles

The situation is equally dire in higher education. Visiting professors and contract teachers often face "oppressive conditions," earning no more than $300 USD per month in private universities. This lack of financial security drives many talented educators away.
