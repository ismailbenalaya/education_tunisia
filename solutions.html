The Path Forward

Comprehensive, multi-faceted reform is the only way to restore quality.

Expert Recommendations

Experts emphasize that fixing Tunisia’s education system requires more than just money—it requires structural change.

  • 1. Secure Funding & Accountability

    Reverse budget cuts and end chronic underinvestment. Launch an "urgent infrastructure audit" to replace crumbling walls and roofs.

  • 2. Decentralize & Engage

    Devolve budgets to school principals to bypass bureaucratic delays. Adopt a "whole-of-system" approach involving parents, unions, and NGOs.

  • 3. Modernize Curricula

    Reinstate broad, secular curricula emphasizing critical thinking. Align education with economic realities to reduce graduate unemployment.

  • 4. Social Dialogue

    Organize a broad national dialogue (akin to 2013) to forge consensus. Commit every party to transparent implementation.

  • 5. Support Teachers

    Improve pay and career prospects to keep talented educators in public service. Hire more qualified teachers for rural areas.

Conclusion

By combining increased investment with community engagement and innovative pedagogy, Tunisia can begin to meet its citizens’ high expectations for education. The focus must be on safety, quality, and inclusion.
