Key Challenges

Urban-Rural Inequality

Schools are concentrated near the coast. Interior regions like Sidi Bouzid face long commutes and fewer qualified teachers.

Aging Infrastructure

Education's share of spending fell by ~27% between 2010 and 2023. The 2025 collapse of a school wall tragically exposed this neglect.

Curriculum Misalignment

Many degrees lead to non-existent public sector jobs. Only about 25% of university graduates find work.

Private Shift

Private primary enrollment has more than doubled since 2015 (to 9.1%), exacerbating inequality.

PISA Rankings

In the 2015 OECD PISA tests, Tunisia ranked 66th out of 70 countries, with most students falling below the minimum proficiency threshold. Two-thirds of secondary graduates fail to reach minimal competency.
